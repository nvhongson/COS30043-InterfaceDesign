Korea Cuisine
Korean cuisine is defined by its bold flavors, fermented ingredients, and emphasis on balance between spicy, sweet, sour, and savory elements. A typical Korean meal consists of rice, soup, grilled meats, and an assortment of side dishes (banchan), including the iconic kimchi.

Korean food features fermented ingredients like gochujang (red chili paste), doenjang (soybean paste), and ganjang (soy sauce), giving its dishes a unique depth of flavor. Popular cooking methods include grilling (for BBQ), stewing, stir-frying, and fermenting.
