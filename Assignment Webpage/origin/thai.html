Thai Cuisine
Thai cuisine is celebrated for its bold flavors, aromatic herbs, and perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements.
Rooted in fresh ingredients and fragrant spices, Thai dishes often include lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, chili, fish sauce, and coconut milk.
Thai food is heavily influenced by Chinese, Indian, and Southeast Asian cooking styles, with popular cooking techniques such as stir-frying, grilling, steaming, and slow-cooking.
Thai meals typically include rice or noodles, accompanied by soups, curries, and salads.
